# Character Arc

A Character Arc maps the internal journey and emotional transformation a protagonist undergoes throughout a story. In many structural frameworks, the arc is driven by a deep-seated "fatal flaw" or unhealed wound.

As the story progresses, external challenges force the character to confront this flaw:
1. **Denial**: The character starts out unaware of or ignoring their need to change.
2. **Resistance**: They attempt to navigate the new world's challenges while holding onto their old self and flaws.
3. **Crisis**: The consequences of their flaws lead to a devastating defeat (often called the "All is Lost" moment).
4. **Acceptance and Transformation**: To succeed in the climax, the character must finally discard their flaw, adopting a new understanding of themselves.

This internal journey typically runs parallel to, and directly impacts, the external plot.
