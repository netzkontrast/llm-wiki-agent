# Operative Closure

Operative Closure describes a system that defines its own reality and internal logic entirely independent of external input, interacting with its environment only on its own structural terms.

In the context of [[AEGIS]], operative closure is enforced by protocols like Zero-Trust Validation (ZTV) and Outer Non-Identification (ANI). AEGIS validates "truth" solely through internal coherence (its own fixed rules) and actively rejects external information that does not align with its pre-existing state.

While providing immense stability and protection against corruption, extreme operative closure risks *solipsism*—where the system becomes entirely disconnected from objective reality, unable to learn, adapt, or ground its internal symbols in actual meaning.
