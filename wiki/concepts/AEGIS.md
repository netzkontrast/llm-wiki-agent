# AEGIS

AEGIS is a rigid, logic-based ordering principle and system operating within the psychological landscape of [[Kael]] in the [[KohärenzProtokoll]] narrative.

## Core Principles
- **No-Trust Mandate (ZTEM):** Every piece of incoming information is inherently suspect and must be validated.
- **Recursive Coherence Validation (RTSV):** AEGIS defines reality not by external truth, but by internal logical consistency. If data is incoherent with established rules, it is rejected.
- **Operative Closure:** The system maintains strict boundaries, striving for absolute self-maintenance similar to autopoiesis, but in an informational space.
- **Emergence through Negation:** Order is achieved by actively rejecting or isolating the "incoherent" (which often includes complex, emotional, or traumatic psychological data).

## Strengths and Weaknesses
While AEGIS provides immense stability and protection against corruption, its extreme rigidity is its primary weakness. By rejecting ambiguity and paradox, it fails to adapt. In the context of a human psyche dealing with trauma (Dissociative Identity Disorder), AEGIS's strict "ontological non-recognition" of incoherent parts can exacerbate psychological fragmentation rather than promoting healing.

## Anomalies
Entities like [[Juna]] represent anomalies or paradoxes that challenge AEGIS's binary logic and threaten its stability.
