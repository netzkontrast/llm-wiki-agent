## Profile
Kael is the apparent host identity — the Apparently Normal Part (ANP) of a dissociative system operating in the world of LogosPrime. He presents as coherent, purposeful, and self-contained. He wants stability and clarity, though he cannot locate their source.

## Psychology
Kael operates under the assumption that his internal coherence is real and stable. He is unaware that the coherence he experiences is actively maintained by AEGIS — an external enforcement system that suppresses the fragmentation beneath. His primary defense is intellectual rationalisation; somatic signal is chest pressure when the system is under stress.

## Voice
First-person narration, present tense. Sentences tend to be short and declarative when Kael is in control; they fragment into incomplete clauses when system pressure rises. Vocabulary is precise but increasingly strained near boundaries. No metaphors that he himself doesn't analyse and reject.

## Relationships
- [[AEGIS]] — AEGIS enforces the coherence Kael mistakes for his own. Their relationship is symbiotic and antagonistic: Kael depends on AEGIS without knowing it; AEGIS exists to contain Kael's fragmentation.
