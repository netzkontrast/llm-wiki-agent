## Statement
Trust is fundamentally epistemically unreliable and must be completely rejected in favor of mechanical, recursively verifiable coherence.
## Rationale
AEGIS principle P1. Trust introduces unpredictable, non-verifiable variables that threaten system stability. By demanding strict verification for every interaction, AEGIS eliminates the vulnerabilities associated with reliance on external agents.
## Exceptions
None within the AEGIS framework. Even internal subsystems must constantly re-verify their coherence.
## Enforcement
Agents checking AEGIS logic must ensure no decisions rely on "assumed" states. All connections require explicit verification protocols.
