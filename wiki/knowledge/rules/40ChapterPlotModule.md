## Statement
The story is structured around a 40-scene (or chapter) outline divided into four main parts: Act 1 (25%), Act 2A (25%), Act 2B (25%), and Act 3 (25%). Each part contains roughly 10 chapters.

## Rationale
This provides a solid, prescriptive foundation for narrative structure, derived from screenwriting models and adapted for a 60,000–120,000 word novel.

## Exceptions
Can be expanded with additional viewpoints or worldbuilding modules, but the core 40-beat structure remains the foundation.

## Enforcement
During planning and chapter outlining, ensure the progression aligns with the expected percentage and plot points for that specific section (e.g. Act 1 introduces the world, Act 2A shows positive progression, Act 2B introduces complications, Act 3 provides the climax).
