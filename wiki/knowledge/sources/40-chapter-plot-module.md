## Summary
This document outlines a prescriptive 40-scene (or 40-chapter) plot structure for a novel, based on screenwriting paradigms. It divides the narrative into four equal acts: Act 1, Act 2A, Act 2B, and Act 3, each constituting 25% of the story.

## Key Facts
- The structure is divided into four parts: Act 1, Act 2A, Act 2B, and Act 3.
- Each act represents approximately 25% of the story.
- It aims to map out roughly 40 scenes/chapters.
- Act 1 introduces the protagonist, ordinary world, and the inciting incident.
- Act 2A represents positive action in the new world.
- Act 2B represents negative action and complications.
- Act 3 is the climax and resolution.
- It mentions optional Prolog and Epilogue chapters.
