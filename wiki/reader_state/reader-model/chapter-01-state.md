## Reader Experience
After Chapter 1, the reader should feel: oriented but uneasy. They know where they are (LogosPrime) and who is narrating (Kael), but the sterility of the environment produces discomfort rather than safety. The precision of Kael's narration reads as controlled rather than calm. Something is being held in place.

## Disclosure Strategy
**Revealed:** Kael exists, LogosPrime is his world, the environment is sterile and geometrically precise.
**Withheld:** AEGIS exists, the fragmentation exists, the cage nature of LogosPrime's coherence.
**Misdirected:** The absence of shadows is presented as aesthetic fact, not as a clue about the nature of coherence-enforcement.

The cage motif is planted via environmental detail — the reader registers it sensory-aesthetically but not yet symbolically. No dramatic irony is active yet: the reader knows only what Kael knows.
