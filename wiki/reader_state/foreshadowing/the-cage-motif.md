## Description
The cage motif foreshadows the revelation that LogosPrime's coherence is not freedom but confinement — that the walls Kael cannot see are walls nonetheless. The motif is introduced subtly in Chapter 1 through architectural detail and Kael's unexamined acceptance of boundaries he has never questioned.

## Planting
In Chapter 1, Kael describes the geometry of LogosPrime with admiration: the right angles, the precise boundaries between spaces. He notes — without comment — that he has never seen a door that opens outward. The image is planted as environmental detail, not yet as symbol.

## Reinforcement
Intermediate appearances will reinforce the motif through:
- Kael noticing other boundaries he accepts without questioning
- The mirror motif intersecting: Kael begins to see AEGIS's enforcement as a kind of boundary-drawing
- Physical compression of LogosPrime's described space as pressure builds

## Resolution
The cage motif resolves (or fails to resolve) at the Goedel Gambit: does exposing the cage dissolve it, or merely make it visible? The resolution status is intentionally left open until the manuscript stage determines the emotional register of the ending.
