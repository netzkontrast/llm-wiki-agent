# Overview

*This page is maintained by the LLM. It is updated on every ingest to reflect the current synthesis across all sources.*

The wiki bridges narrative theory with complex psychological and systemic worldbuilding.

On a structural level, the framework relies on a [[NovelStructure]] inspired by a 40-scene model that maps a protagonist's [[CharacterArc]] from flawed beginnings to transformation.

Within this narrative shell lies the [[KohärenzProtokoll]], a story exploring the deeply fragmented psyche of its protagonist, [[Kael]], who suffers from trauma and Dissociative Identity Disorder. Controlling this inner landscape is [[AEGIS]], an uncompromising, rigid system that uses strict logic to enforce internal coherence, completely rejecting any "incoherent" trauma or paradoxes. By maintaining isolated [[CoreWorlds]], AEGIS seeks absolute stability, but its "ontological non-recognition" of complex emotions actively hinders psychological healing. This tension between systemic order and adaptive psychological integration is embodied by the anomaly [[Juna]], who represents the messy, flexible reality that AEGIS cannot compute.
