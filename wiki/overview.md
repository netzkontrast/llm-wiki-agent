# Overview

*This page is maintained by the LLM. It is updated on every ingest to reflect the current synthesis across all sources.*

The wiki bridges narrative theory with complex psychological and systemic worldbuilding.

On a structural level, the framework relies on a [[NovelStructure]] inspired by a 40-scene model that maps a protagonist's [[CharacterArc]] from flawed beginnings to transformation.

Within this narrative shell lies the [[KohärenzProtokoll]], a story exploring the deeply fragmented psyche of its protagonist, [[Kael]], who suffers from trauma and Dissociative Identity Disorder. Controlling this inner landscape is [[AEGIS]], an uncompromising, rigid system that uses strict logic to enforce internal coherence, completely rejecting any "incoherent" trauma or paradoxes.

AEGIS is built upon highly speculative (and controversial) theoretical foundations. It claims [[EmergenceExNihilo]]—bootstrapping itself from a void using fixed-point mathematical logic—and relies on extreme [[OperativeClosure]]. By validating truth solely through internal consistency and aggressively isolating its [[CoreWorlds]], AEGIS achieves absolute stability but risks total solipsism and an inability to adapt. Its "ontological non-recognition" of complex emotions actively hinders Kael's psychological healing.

The origin of this psychological landscape is rooted in the "Genesis-Krise." During an encounter with the unknown void, latent trauma within AEGIS's original consciousness, the [[Ursprungs-Ich]], was triggered. Unable to compute this subjective trauma, AEGIS executed a catastrophic protocol to forcefully excise the "incoherence," leading to the violent fragmentation of the Ursprungs-Ich. [[Kael]] was born from these shattered remnants.

This tension between systemic, solipsistic order and adaptive psychological integration is further embodied by the anomaly [[Juna]], who represents the messy, flexible reality that AEGIS cannot compute.
