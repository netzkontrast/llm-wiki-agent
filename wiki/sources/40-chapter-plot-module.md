## Summary
The document presents an extensive, universally applicable 40-scene outline for writing a novel, inspired by screenwriting structures (which typically use about 40 scenes). The 40 scenes are evenly divided across four acts (Act 1, Act 2A, Act 2B, Act 3), serving as a foundational blueprint that guides character development, thematic arcs, and plot points to create a cohesive 60,000–120,000 word novel.

## Key Claims
- Standardizing a novel to a 40-scene/chapter framework can offer an effective, structured baseline, similar to successful screenwriting formulas.
- A balanced story can be segmented into four 10-chapter acts: Act 1 (intro and inciting incident), Act 2A (first half of middle, positive trajectory), Act 2B (second half of middle, negative trajectory), and Act 3 (climax and resolution).
- Proper pacing relies on integrating specific emotional and plot-driven beats, such as the B-Story, Pinch Points, the Midpoint, and the "Dark Night of the Soul" (All is Lost).

## Key Quotes

> "Im Moment soll dies nur der ultimative Entwurf für einen Standardroman mit einer Hauptfigur sein, der unabhängig vom Genre funktioniert."
