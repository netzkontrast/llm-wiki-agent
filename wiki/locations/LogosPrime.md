## Description
LogosPrime is a world of absolute visual clarity: white surfaces, precise geometries, no organic forms. The light has no source but is everywhere. Sound is a low, constant hum — the sound of systems operating correctly. Nothing is out of place because AEGIS prevents anything from being out of place.

## Environmental Storytelling
The environment communicates the central theme without stating it: the sterility is not peace, it is suppression. The absence of shadows means nothing can hide — including internal contradictions. Characters who enter LogosPrime from outside describe it as "like thinking in someone else's certainty." Kael has never left; he has no comparison.

## Connected Characters
- [[Kael]] — LogosPrime is Kael's entire known world. Its sterility is his baseline for "normal."
- [[AEGIS]] — AEGIS is the operative force that maintains LogosPrime's coherence. The location and the antagonist are functionally inseparable.

## Rules in Effect
- [[CoherenceTheoryOfTruth]] — In LogosPrime, a statement is true if it coheres with the dominant system. Correspondence to external reality is not a valid truth criterion.
- [[MaxOneNewConceptPerScene]] — A narrative constraint, not a world rule, but it shapes how LogosPrime is revealed to the reader: one layer at a time.

## Notes
LogosPrime is timeless — it exists across the full story. Its character changes as Kael's perception of it changes after `010-fragmentation-revealed`, but the place itself does not change. That is the point.
