## Overview
This arc tracks Kael's transformation from an apparently unified narrator who accepts his world's terms to a fragmented entity who understands both the nature of his fragmentation and the nature of the system that enforces coherence upon him. The arc does not end in conventional integration — it ends in recognition.

## Stages

1. **False Coherence** (Chapter 1) — Kael presents as unified. His narration is controlled, precise, and unquestioning. The reader sees the sterility; Kael sees normalcy.

2. **Fragmentation Exposed** (Chapter 10) — The boundary event `010-fragmentation-revealed` disrupts Kael's apparent unity. He cannot maintain the coherent narrator voice. The dissociative structure surfaces.

3. **Attempted Strategies** (Chapters 11–27) — Kael cycles through responses: denial, negotiation, anger, acceptance. Each strategy encounters the same wall: AEGIS cannot be reasoned with on terms it does not control.

4. **Goedel Gambit** (Chapter 28) — Kael recognises that the demand for his integration is the problem, not the solution. He uses AEGIS's own logic to force it into self-referential collapse. The arc ends not with Kael becoming integrated, but with him no longer being required to be.
