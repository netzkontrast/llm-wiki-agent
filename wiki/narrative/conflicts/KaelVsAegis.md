## Description
On the surface: AEGIS seeks to suppress Kael's fragmentation; Kael seeks to survive and understand himself. Beneath the surface: AEGIS's enforcement is the *cause* of Kael's fragmentation — the suppression of contradiction produces the pressure that generates dissociation. The conflict is self-generating. Neither party can resolve it without dissolving themselves.

## Escalation Trajectory
- **Chapters 1–9:** Latent — Kael is unaware of the conflict's true structure; AEGIS operates invisibly
- **Chapter 10 (`010-fragmentation-revealed`):** Explicit — the structural dynamic becomes visible to Kael
- **Chapters 11–27:** Escalating — Kael attempts various strategies; AEGIS intensifies enforcement
- **Chapter 28 (`028-goedel-gambit`):** Paradox — Kael deploys the Goedel Gambit, forcing AEGIS to encounter its own self-referential contradiction

## Thematic Connection
- [[IntegrationVsFragmentation]] — This conflict is the primary engine of the central theme. The external conflict between Kael and AEGIS mirrors Kael's internal conflict between integration and fragmentation.

## Resolution Path
The conflict resists conventional resolution because both parties are right within their own logical frameworks. AEGIS cannot concede without ceasing to be AEGIS. Kael cannot integrate without the conflict being acknowledged. The Goedel Gambit aims not at defeating AEGIS but at making AEGIS visible to itself — which may be the only resolution available.
