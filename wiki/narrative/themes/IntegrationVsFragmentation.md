## Statement
Integration and fragmentation are not opposites to be resolved — they are a false binary maintained by a system that benefits from the opposition.

## Expression
- **Plot:** AEGIS enforces integration; Kael experiences fragmentation. The conflict between them drives every scene.
- **Character:** Kael's internal dissociation is the theme embodied. His arc is not to "become integrated" but to recognise that the demand for integration is itself the wound.
- **Setting:** LogosPrime's sterile coherence is integration made visible as a built environment — and shown to be oppressive rather than healing.

## Motifs
- **The cage** — walls that are also definitions; the boundary between integrated and fragmented is itself a kind of imprisonment. See [[the-cage-motif]].
- **The mirror** — self-reflection as both discovery and trap; Kael sees himself in AEGIS and cannot look away.
- **Breath** — the somatic signal for system pressure; breath is what escapes control even inside LogosPrime.

## Evolution
The theme appears first as an environmental fact (LogosPrime's sterility), then as a personal crisis (Kael's fragmentation revealed), then as a philosophical problem (can fragmentation be integrated without being destroyed?), and finally as a structural insight (the demand for integration was always the problem, not the solution).

## Dialectic
No counter_theme is currently assigned. The theme is internally dialectical — integration and fragmentation oppose each other within the single theme rather than requiring a separate counter-theme page.

## Notes
Central theme. All other themes and conflicts connect back to this one. The motifs (the-cage, the-mirror, breath) recur across chapters and should be tracked in their respective foreshadowing/motif pages.
